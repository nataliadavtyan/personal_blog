Flexbox stretched my image

For the About page I applied display: flex and my image turned from round shape to oval even though I set fixed height and width to it.

What caused the problem?

There are two items in this container: a round image and a text container. In the mobile version they are on separate lines, but when the window is wider, display: flex is applied. As a result the image is vertically stretched.

Once the window becomes wide enough, the image also gets its shape back. The reason this happened is that the text was trying to get the space it needed and thus pushing the image for that extra space.

How was it fixed?

A lot of resources online suggested to apply align-self: center to the image but it did not change anything. Then with the help of the Scrimba's community on Discord I was able to prevent the image from stretching.

Mist from Discord suggested to give the image flex-basis: 11.375em (size of the image) and flex-shrink: 0.

He also provided a great explanation to this: "The key here is the flex-basis and flex-shrink properties. flex-basis is like saying "this is ideally the size I want. flex-shrink is saying "this is the minimum amount to shrink it". By setting flex-shrink to 0, you're saying, "If at all possible, DO NOT shrink this element"."

It worked.
